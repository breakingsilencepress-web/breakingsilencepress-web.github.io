Context

This judicial opinion confirms that government imposed patent secrecy orders can plausibly cause real economic harm to inventors, including loss of commercialization opportunities, foreign filing rights, and market access. Although the court rejected constitutional and equitable challenges, it explicitly allowed the inventors' statutory compensation claim to move forward. This document supports Chapter One's central claim that secrecy mechanisms function as instruments of suppression while remaining largely shielded from constitutional accountability.
