Context

This document establishes the legal framework allowing the U.S. government to issue secrecy orders on patent applications that might harm national security. It defines procedures for inspection, secrecy orders, appeals, and foreign filing licenses, and sets out responsibilities for inventors and their assignees. It directly supports Chapter 1’s claim regarding systemic suppression of transformative inventions through government secrecy orders, demonstrating how legal mechanisms can prevent public disclosure of potentially disruptive technologies.
