Verification Metadata

  • Document title: Lag (1971:1078) om försvarsuppfinningar — Swedish Defence Inventions Act
  • Issuing Authority / Court / Agency: Swedish Government / Justitiedepartementet (Ministry of Justice)
  • Original date: December 17, 1971 (Amended through SFS 2016:195)
  • Legal status: Current; amended and in force, governs secrecy, patenting, and control of military-relevant inventions in Sweden.
  • Original Source Institution: Regeringskansliet / Lagrummet.se

Context

The Swedish Defence Inventions Act establishes legal control over inventions related to military materiel ("försvarsuppfinningar"). Key provisions include:

  • Definitions and Scope (§1–4): Covers inventions that pertain to war materiel. Swedish-origin or resident-owned inventions may not be publicly disclosed until reviewed for secrecy.
  • Review Authorities (§2–3, §5–6): Försvarets materielverk (FMV) evaluates patent applications for secrecy; the Granskningsnämnden makes final decisions.
  • Secrecy and Control (§6–18): Provides timelines for determining secrecy; allows government intervention for state use; includes procedures for foreign-origin inventions with Swedish relevance.
  • Legal Consequences (§19–24): Enforces confidentiality obligations, sets penalties for violations, and specifies appeal routes (government and Patent & Market Court).
  • Compensation: Inventors are entitled to reasonable remuneration if secrecy restrictions or government takeover affect the invention (§14, §16).
  • Transitional Provisions: Updates and amendments maintain continuity for ongoing patent cases and court proceedings (1993:1407, 2016:195).

Significance:
This law codifies a formal mechanism to classify, control, and withhold sensitive technological knowledge in Sweden, paralleling the U.S. "Invention Secrecy Act" model. It is critical in tracing institutional suppression of dual-use or transformative technologies, particularly in national defense contexts.
