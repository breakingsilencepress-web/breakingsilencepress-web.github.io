A Secrecy System Hiding Inventions

For decades, U.S. national security policy has enabled the suppression of civilian inventions â€” blocking patents from publication, prohibiting foreign filing, and erasing technologies from the public record. This is not an exception. It is a system.

The Evidence

Hundreds of U.S. patents have been placed under secrecy orders using the Invention Secrecy Act of 1951, often renewed indefinitely under claims of national security â€” without public oversight or transparency.

Blocked from Publication

Inventions are withheld from the public record, sometimes for decades, preventing disclosure, peer review, and civilian use.

No Foreign Filing

Inventors are legally prohibited from filing or discussing their work outside the United States.

Complete Secrecy

In some cases, even the existence of an invention is classified, creating permanent informational blackouts.
