A Secrecy System Hiding Inventions

For decades, U.S. national security policy has enabled the suppression of civilian inventions — blocking patents from publication, prohibiting foreign filing, and erasing technologies from the public record. This is not an exception. It is a system.

The Evidence

Hundreds of U.S. patents have been placed under secrecy orders using the Invention Secrecy Act of 1951, often renewed indefinitely under claims of national security — without public oversight or transparency.

Blocked from Publication

Inventions are withheld from the public record, sometimes for decades, preventing disclosure, peer review, and civilian use.

No Foreign Filing

Inventors are legally prohibited from filing or discussing their work outside the United States.

Complete Secrecy

In some cases, even the existence of an invention is classified, creating permanent informational blackouts.

Breaking Silence documents how this secrecy regime was built, how it operates, and how thousands of inventions quietly disappeared inside it — using declassified policy, government statistics, and international secrecy laws.

Timeline of Secrecy

2007-2009

Invention Filed & Secrecy Imposed

2007: Budimir Damnjanović files patent for infrared missile countermeasure (USPTO No. 11/881,492)

2008: Referred for national security review under 35 U.S.C. § 181

Jan 2009: Secrecy order imposed - patent removed from public view

Evidence: USPTO records & Federal court complaint (QR 1.1, 1.3)

2009-2012

Forced Silence & Denied Compensation

2009-2012: Invention suppressed for 3+ years without hearing or appeal

Jun-Jul 2012: Compensation claim filed under 35 U.S.C. § 183

2012: U.S. Air Force denies compensation claim

Nov 2013: Secrecy order renewed despite pending dispute

Evidence: Court filings & statutory framework

2014-2015

Lawsuit & Secrecy Lifted

May 2014: Lawsuit filed (Case No. 4:14-cv-11920) alleging economic harm, unconstitutional speech restraint, and uncompensated taking

Jan 2015: Secrecy lifted after 6+ years - patent issues as U.S. Patent No. 8,925,438 B1

Sep 2015: Court allows compensation claim to proceed

Evidence: Court complaint & USPTO records (QR 1.1, 1.3)

2017-2025

Resolution & Systemic Pattern

Mar 2017: Case settled confidentially - no precedent set

2021-2025: Government data shows rising secrecy orders (6,543 active in 2025)

2025: Case documented as representative example in Chapter One

Evidence: Federation of American Scientists & Chapter One (QR 1.4)

This free sample chapter introduces the legal and institutional foundations of invention secrecy using court rulings, statutory authority, and verified patent records. Rather than speculation, Chapter One establishes how secrecy operates as a formal system—enforced through law, courts, and policy.

    A U.S. federal ruling illustrating how secrecy defenses are asserted and upheld when classified technologies intersect with civil litigation.
  • U.S. Patent Secrecy Statute (35 U.S.C. Chapter 17)
    The statutory authority allowing the U.S. government to impose secrecy orders on patent applications for national security reasons.
  • Infrared Missile Countermeasure Patent (US 8,925,438 B1)
    A real, declassified patent demonstrating how advanced defensive technologies can be delayed, restricted, or partially obscured.
  • U.S. Invention Secrecy Activity — Archived Snapshot (2025)
    Preserved government data documenting the scale and persistence of active secrecy orders.

    An international statute showing that invention secrecy is not uniquely American, but part of a broader global security framework.
