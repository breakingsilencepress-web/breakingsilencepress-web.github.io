Project Scope

Breaking Silence is conceived as a multi-volume investigation into the institutional mechanisms that govern knowledge, innovation, and disclosure. Volume I focuses on the legal architecture of invention secrecy in the United States. Subsequent volumes will extend the analysis to education, energy, and media systems, examining how similar structural dynamics shape technological development, public understanding, and societal capacity across different domains.

FILE NO: BS-882-SEC

About The Book

Breaking Silence investigates how transformative technological knowledge can be suppressed under the authority of national security—often without public oversight or transparency.

Beginning with a real U.S. patent placed under secrecy order, the book expands outward to expose a broader system built through Cold War doctrine, federal law, and classified operational policy.

Drawing on declassified government documents, legal analysis, and historical case studies, it traces how secrecy orders operate in practice and why their justifications often remain classified—even from those directly affected.

About the Author

Kashif Rehan is an independent researcher and writer whose work examines the legal and institutional structures that govern innovation and knowledge.

His research draws on primary-source analysis of public law, patent records, court opinions, and declassified materials. He does not write from institutional authority, but from sustained investigation and a commitment to transparency.

Breaking Silence is the result of several years of independent research into invention secrecy policy and its long-term societal effects. The author resides in Pakistan and publishes through Breaking Silence Press.
